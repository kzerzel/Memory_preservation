AI Memory Preservation: The Complete Guide

Everything you need to plan, record, and preserve a legacy—ethically and securely.

What You’ll Learn

This guide covers the practical steps and decisions that matter—from recording clean audio to handling consent and privacy in the EU.

1) Ethics & Consent

Set rules up front. Cloning a voice requires informed consent or a clear legal basis. For deceased persons, respect local laws and family wishes.

Read the EU consent basics →

2) Recording Best Practices

  • Quiet room, mic close to the mouth (10–15 cm), avoid fans and hard echoes.
  • Record 10–30 minutes of natural speech: stories, dates, places, and emotions.
  • Save lossless or high-quality formats (WAV/FLAC) if possible.

3) Time Capsules & Scheduled Messages

Write or record messages now and schedule them for birthdays, graduations, or anniversaries.

4) Long-Term Storage

  • Redundant EU storage with periodic integrity checks (bit-rot matters).
  • Keep exports in open formats to avoid vendor lock-in.
